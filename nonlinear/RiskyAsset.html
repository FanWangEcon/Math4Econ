Risky Assets and Different States of the World

Uncertainty

Suppose there are 2 states of the world tomorrow, in one state of the world, the economy is booming, the other not so great.
If you deposited money into a savings account at Bank of America, your earnings in the good and bad state are likely to be very similar.
If you bought stocks, you might make a lot of money when the economy is booming, but lose money when the economy is not doing well.

Differential Returns Depending on the State of the World

Let us formalize things. A household can save in safe asset and for each dollar saved, get dollar tomorrow. Alternatively, a household can invest in risky asset. In the good state of the world tomorrow, the household will receive back for each dollar invested. In the bad state of the world tomorrow, the household will receive nothing--lose all.
